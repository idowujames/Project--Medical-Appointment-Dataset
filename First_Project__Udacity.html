Project: Investigate a Dataset - Medical Appointment No Shows¶

Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset contains information from 100k plus medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

The variables represented in this dataset are:

01 - PatientId : Identification of a patient , 02 - AppointmentID : Identification of each appointment , 03 - Gender : Male or Female , 04 - AppointmentDay : The day of the actual appointment , 05 - ScheduledDay : The day someone called or registered the appointment , 06 - Age : Patient's Age , 07 - Neighbourhood : Were the appointments is taking place , 08 - Scholarship : True of False(Bolsa Famlia Scholarship), 09 - Hipertension : True of False , 10 - Diabetes : True of False , 11 - Alcoholism : True of False , 12 - Handcap : True of False , 13 - SMS_received : 1 or more messages sent to the patient , 14 - No-show : True of False

Questions for Analysis¶

*1) How much of a role does age play in whether a person will show up for their appointment**

Rational: Younger people tend to prioritize other things over their health. I will try to findout if younger people who are above the age of parental guidance have a higher percentage of missing appointments than other age groups.

*2) How much of a role does the time difference(amount of days) between schedule day and the actual date of the appointment play in whether a person will show up for their appointment**

Rational: According to the behavioural sciences, the time intervel between intention and execution plays a significant role in human behaviour. I intend to find out whether the longer the time difference between the day the appointment was made and actual date of appointment makes any significant difference in the patient's showing up for it.

No further information is given by dataset creators.

Conclusions¶

Based of the two questions posed at the begining of this analysis, it appears, the age group of a person is a good indicator of whether they will show-up for their doctor's appointment.
It also seems that the time difference(amount of days) between schedule date and appointment doesn't have a strong influence on whether a person will show up for their appointment. Anything outside of a roughly a week , the time difference no longer becomes a good indicator. It seems not to matter much if the days between the schedule date and appointment date is a month or five months.



This analysis didn't cross check it's hypotesis against factors like whether the patient is handicapped or whether they have any of the following issues hipertension,diabetes,alcoholism. This are factors that might have effects on the hypotesis and results and they need to be further explored.
